Keynotes

Don Bora
Co-Founder & Principal of Technology, Eight Bit Studios

Don Bora has been a professional software developer in Chicago since 1990. In his career, Don has started 6 companies one of which he sold and two of which are still alive and making a lot of noise.

Don is a co-founder of Eight Bit Studios, a leading web and mobile application firm in Chicago. At Eight Bit, we blend design, U/X, and software development collaboratively to deliver digital products to our customs. If we do it right, everyone has a great time and walks away with a smile on their face.

Don is also a co-founder of Mobile Makers, an immersive mobile bootcamp based in Chicago. Taking adult students from never-coded to app-in-the-app-store in 8 weeks, Don is also the lead instructor and an aggressive community connector for his students. Don also spearheaded curriculum development for Mobile Makers high school iniative piloted with 7 Chicago area high schools.

A graduate of New Mexico State University with degrees in Computer Science and Linguistics, Don is very involved with technology education. An outspoken advocate of women in technology, Don regularly mentors young technically minded women through both the educations and careers.

For more, keep up with Don on Twitter as @dbora.

Ben Lister
Senior UI Designer, Sprout Social

Ben built his first website, a Nirvana fan page, in the late 90’s (on GeoCities) and hasn’t looked back since. He works at Sprout Social where he designs, prototypes and builds interfaces relied on daily by social media teams at many of the World’s leading brands and agencies. Ben is an avid White Sox, Sports Car, Running, Cooking and Craft Beer enthusiast and lives in Chicago with his wife Erin and a rambunctious Whippet named Devo.

For more, keep up with Ben at benlister.net or on Twitter as @benclister.

Creating a Scalable Image Workflow with SVG

SVG has been one of the hottest buzz words in the web community over the last couple of years but with so many options how do you get started? This talk will explore some of the capabilities, benefits and real world techniques you can use today to create a SVG workflow for your next project. Topics covered will include: SVG Maps, Icon Fonts, Optimizations, Animations and the Pitfalls.

Brian Montana
Interactive Developer, closerlook inc

Brian Montana has a Masters of Fine Art in New Media and is currently working as an Interactive Developer at closerlook inc, he’s worked with clients such as P&G, Merck, Takeda, Blue Bunny, AMA, Astellas, Ricola, Paris Presents and more. Brian wields technology and an artistic gaze, merging aesthetics and function into his process to harmonize communication between development and design. Often you can find Brian at a museum or art gallery continually exploring his love of education, exhibitions and art.

For more, keep up with Brian at brianmontana.net or on Twitter as @brianmontanaweb.

Design Through the Lens of Developer Eyes

The discussions in digital workflow have focused on the expectation of the designer communicating and thinking like a developer, an artifact of the waterfall workflow. The dynamic of the emerging workflow, repositions the conversations away from the canon and invites others to use empathy when collaborating on projects. Within this talk I’ll cover how I, as a developer, communicate through the perspective of a designer and how making a designers life easier makes the current, and future, projects easier to manage.
